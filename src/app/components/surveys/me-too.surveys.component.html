Trigger/Content Warning: Sexual assault, #MeToo movement

Note: Your responses to this survey are completely anonymous. Names, email addresses, or any other type of personal information are not being collected. Not all questions may apply to you, so do not feel like you have to respond with a concrete answer. It's okay to say "I don't know" or not answer if you do not have answer.

We’ve all heard the statistic “One in five women will be sexually assaulted in college.” Do we understand what goes into this statistic, however? This isn’t a game of roulette at all. These are real people. These are people with complex and variegated emotions and mental states. These people may be your best friend, your neighbor or your roommate. These people won’t always be women. These people won't always be men, either. These people won’t always be adults. These are real events that impact them, perhaps for the rest of their life.

This study aims to analyze the language used when talking about the #MeToo movement, and delve deeper into understanding the sentiments and emotions of such a movement, and to finally demonstrate that sexual assault is more than just a statistic - it is a lived experienced and the attitudes that surround it heavily influence our day-to-day interactions.

By participating in this survey, you are helping launch this very important project in changing the discourse surrounding sexual asault. Thank you for filling out this survey. Your contribution is valued.
