Mission Statement

The Pomegranate Society aims towards gender equality and the elimination of gender-based discrimination, with a special focus on deterring and eradicating sexual abuse, crimes, and violence. The aim is to address the stigma around sexual assault, particularly in the cultural context of MENA and South Asia, and open a platform for discussion about these topics. The goal is to produce constructive discourse surrounding the topic, analyze the multidimensional factors that impact victims of these crimes, and validate the stories for voices that may otherwise not be heard. Feminism, justice, and healing come in different forms, and there is no story that is a copy of another. We are here to celebrate the uniqueness that each one of us possesses and take joy in being outspoken, unapologetic, and sincere.

Call to Action

Productive conversation around sexual assault is required now more than ever. A call to action on how to handle these complex situations and interactions need to be heard. Your stories deserve to be told and shared on your terms.
Restorative justice can be a very imperative tool in such productive conversation. This form of justice aims to repair the harm caused by crime. When victims, survivors, perpetrators, and the communities meet together as a whole to discuss solutions, innovative and transformational solutions can possibly be created.
