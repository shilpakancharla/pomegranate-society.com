The moon, she weeps.

by Allie Todd

The moon, she weeps for me.
She cries for me in the night.
She knows that’s when the memories, they scratch and they claw and they fight,
To take root in my dreams,
And to keep me up all night.


The moon, she weeps for me. When the sun goes down she knows she won’t be enough for me.
She tries to keep the tides at bay,
But they come and they take me away,
Piece by piece.


The moon, she weeps for me.
She weeps as I can barely remember how to breathe.
She weeps and weeps all night it seems.
As I toss and I turn,
Engulfed by the memories of his unwanted hand on me.


The moon, she weeps for me.
For the first time though,
I feel a little stronger.
I do not feel the weight of world upon my shoulders.
Sleep, comes naturally.


The moon, she weeps for me.
It’s a different sort of cry tonight,
She sees me sleeping so peacefully.
The sun, he told her of my strength.
He told her of the grace,
The grace it took to forgive.


So, the moon, the moon, she wept for me,
For years it seemed, but she wept only one last time tonight.
She wept for the growth she’s seen in me.
She wept for the nights I couldn’t seem to quit weeping.
The moon left her strength in me.


Now the moon and the sun rejoice in me.
What a long road behind us,
And oh how many things they have seen.
Their tears dried with mine,
And although the toughest moments are behind,
I will never forget how the moon wept for me.
