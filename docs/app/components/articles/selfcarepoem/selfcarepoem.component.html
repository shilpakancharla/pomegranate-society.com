Self-Care

by Allie Todd

Trigger/content warning: rape.


Self-care, self-care, self-care, they preach
But how will brushing my hair make me forget his hands on me.
Self-care, self-care, self-care, they say
But how will a bubble bath cure the way,
My eyes dart around a room to make sure he isn’t there.


Self-care, self-care, self-care, they yell
Our sisters, our mothers, our therapists
But do they not know,
That self-care won’t remove the image of my rapist.


Do they not know,
That washing my body in fancy soaps
Will not erase the image of my blood stained clothes?
I know they are trying to help!!
But do they not know,
Self-care isn’t the only way to heal my broken heart?


Relax, do something for yourself, they whisper
But when the night falls the only voice I hear, is his.
I can’t see a staircase without thinking of that night,
And I can’t breathe sometimes.
Tell me, how will painting my nails bring me back to life.


Not everyone heals in the same way, I say back.
I can’t light a candle and go back,
Go back to before when I was whole.
But I can write, and I can brush my hair,
And I can take a bath, and I can fight the system.


Self-care alone will not heal us all.
Healing does not come in one shape, one size.
So when they tell you,
Just to take care of yourself, your physical self,
Please, please do it for you.
But don’t forget to take care of your mind too.
